## Patterns of the Top 10%

After analyzing the top 3 competitors, these patterns separate elite church websites from average:

### Pattern 1: Video or Full-Bleed Photography Hero
All top sites use either video heroes (Brown MBC) or full-bleed, high-quality photography (Brandon BC, Pinelake) with white overlaid text. The hero IS the brand statement. No slide carousels. One powerful moment.

### Pattern 2: Three-Path Welcome Framework
The best sites give new visitors 3 clear pathways immediately after the hero: typically **Connect / Grow / Serve** or **Plan Your Visit / Watch Online / Get Involved**. This reduces decision paralysis and guides first-time guests.

### Pattern 3: Content Engine (Weekly Fresh Content)
Top sites publish weekly content — sermons, devotionals, event recaps. This signals life and keeps Google happy. Brown MBC's daily devotional is their #1 SEO driver. The TAB has no regular content cadence.

### Pattern 4: Real Photography Over Stock
Every top competitor uses real photos of their congregation, facilities, and events. Authentic > polished. The TAB already has real photos — they just aren't used effectively.

### Pattern 5: Mobile-First Information Hierarchy
Top sites put the most important info (service times, location, how to visit) within 2 scrolls on mobile. The TAB buries this. A first-time visitor landing on mobile can't find service times without hunting.
