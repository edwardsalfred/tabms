Executive Summary

The strategic landscape and immediate opportunity

Key Findings High Opportunity

Tabernacle Missionary Baptist Church — "The TAB" — operates a Wix-based website that currently scores near the bottom of every measurable category when compared to its regional competitors. The most pressing issue is that the site still carries Wix's default title "My Site," signaling to search engines and visitors alike that the site is unfinished. With 13+ active ministries, a dedicated pastor, and a clear community mission, The TAB has significantly more program depth than its template-level presentation suggests.

The competitive gap is wide — but entirely closeable. The top competitor (Pinelake Church) operates at a professional media-organization level with a custom-built WordPress site, video heroes, and multi-campus presence. However, the more relevant benchmark for D'Iberville is Brandon Baptist Church: a single-campus Mississippi church with a clean Squarespace execution that scores 59/80 — more than double The TAB's estimated 23/80. A targeted redesign can close that gap in one build cycle.

The single biggest opportunity: local SEO. "Church D'Iberville MS" and "Baptist church D'Iberville Mississippi" are low-competition keywords with no dominant local winner. The TAB is positioned to own them the moment it has a properly titled, schema-marked website.

Patterns of the Top 10%

What every winning church website does that The TAB currently doesn't

01
Video or Full-Bleed Photography Hero
Top sites open with a cinematic moment — no carousels, no stock photos. One powerful image or loop video with 12–16 words of white-text headline. The TAB has real photography; it just needs intentional curation.
02
Three-Path Welcome Framework
Connect / Grow / Serve (or Plan Your Visit / Watch / Get Involved). Top sites never leave new visitors guessing. Three clear pathways below the hero eliminates bounce and guides the first-time guest.
03
Weekly Fresh Content Engine
Sermons, devotionals, event recaps — something new every week. Brown MBC's daily devotional is their #1 SEO driver. The TAB's media content and pastor's messages could power this engine.
04
Real Congregation Photography
Authentic > polished. Competitors use real people, real moments, real facilities. The TAB already has excellent event photography — the What's Happening page has dozens of usable images waiting to be featured.
05
Mobile-First Information Hierarchy
Service times, location, and how-to-visit within 2 mobile scrolls. 70%+ of church site traffic is mobile — first-time visitors looking up service times on Sunday morning. This must be frictionless.
06
Social Proof Above the Fold
Top sites quantify community: "7 Campuses," "15,000+ weekly attenders," testimonial quotes, member stories. The TAB's 13+ ministries and active community are an untold story. Numbers and stories build trust.

Typography Pairing

Instrument Serif
for headings

DM Sans for body copy, labels, and navigation. Both free via Google Fonts. Seen in top-tier church and editorial sites — serious but warm.

Animation Style

GSAP ScrollTrigger: fade-up reveals for text, stagger for card grids, parallax on hero photography. Inspired by Pinelake's cinematic homepage scroll.

Avoid: carousels, auto-playing sliders, hover-to-reveal content.

Photography Direction

Pull from the What's Happening gallery — it's full of authentic congregation moments. Hero: wide shot, Sunday service, congregation in worship. Ministry cards: real ministry leaders, not placeholder images.

Tone: joyful, genuine, community-oriented.

What to AVOID (Competitor Data)
  • Photo carousels — all top sites use single hero moments
  • Wix/template visual signatures (floating elements, thin dividers)
  • Cluttered homepage — every top church limits the hero to 1 headline + 1–2 CTAs
  • Broken ministry card images — the current grid damages credibility
  • Generic CTAs — "More About Us Here!" has zero conversion power
  • Vague Facebook/social links — broken social links signal abandonment
